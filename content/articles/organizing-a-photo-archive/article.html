Photo archives tend to become chaotic when every new trip or camera roll introduces a new naming convention.

Use categories sparingly

Top-level categories should stay broad. Film, digital, and travel are enough for many personal archives.

Let albums hold the detail

Albums are where the narrative belongs. They can represent a film stock, a destination, or a shooting session without requiring a new route pattern.

Add metadata only when it helps

EXIF can handle a lot of the technical details automatically. Manual overrides are best reserved for title, caption, tags, and other story-specific information.
